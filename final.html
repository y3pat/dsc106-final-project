StressScape

Welcome to StressScape

College is a chaotic mix of exams, deadlines, job hunting, and the existential dread of pretending to have your life together. But have you ever wondered how stress actually affects you? This interactive journey breaks down the science behind stress—one painfully relatable scenario at a time.

Baseline: The Calm Before the Storm

Before stress kicks in, your body is at rest. Heart rate? Normal. Brain? Functioning. This is your starting point before chaos takes over.

POV:

It's Sunday night. You’re feeling fresh, motivated, and totally ready to have a productive week. You swear you’re going to keep up with your readings this time...

Stroop Test: When Your Brain Betrays You

This test forces you to name the color of a word, rather than reading the word itself. Sounds easy? Try saying the color of the word "RED" when it's written in blue ink. Your brain has to fight its instincts, and this cognitive conflict increases stress levels.

POV:

Your professor just changed the exam format. You’re rereading the same sentence 10 times, but nothing is processing.

First Rest: The Fake Break

After mental overload, your body tries to recover. But stress doesn’t always disappear immediately.

POV:

You take a "quick break" before studying. Next thing you know, it's three hours later, and you’re deep in a conspiracy theory rabbit hole on TikTok.

TMCT: The Math Trauma Test

(actually called the Trier Mental Challenge Test) A timed math challenge under pressure. Nothing says "fun" like solving equations while your brain actively tries to shut down.

POV:

You open your exam, and the first question is: “What is 17 × 8?” Your brain: "Blue?"

Second Rest: Stress Aftermath

Even after the challenge is over, your stress doesn’t immediately fade. This phase tracks how long it takes to calm down.

POV:

You walk out of the exam pretending to be fine, but internally, you’re still replaying every question you got wrong.

Real Opinion: Speaking Up Under Pressure

Public speaking, class discussions, or job interviews—expressing yourself under scrutiny is its own form of stress.

POV:

Your professor randomly calls on you: "What do you think?" Your soul leaves your body.

Rest Between Tests 1

Sometimes, a break isn’t really a break. Your body is still processing the stress from the last challenge, and you’re already bracing yourself for the next one.

POV:

You close your laptop after finishing an assignment, but your mind immediately jumps to the next deadline. You’re not resting, you’re just waiting for the next wave.

Opposite Opinion: Debating Against Yourself

Ever had to argue for a position you don’t believe in? Welcome to the stress of cognitive dissonance.

POV:

You’re in a group project and somehow got assigned to defend why pineapple belongs on pizza even though you hate pineapple on pizza.

Rest Between Tests 2

Another short break—but is it enough? With stress accumulating, this is the moment when fatigue can start taking over.

POV:

You tell yourself you’ll get 8 hours of sleep tonight. Then, suddenly, it’s 2 AM and you’re still awake, scrolling.

Subtract Test: The Countdown to Doom

Quick math, under pressure—like when a professor asks for calculations mid-lecture.

POV:

Professor: "What’s 256 divided by 8?" You: "Uhhhhhhh…"

Post Experiment: Stress Reflection

Now that all the stress tests are done, we can see how stress levels fluctuated. Did they spike? Recover? Linger?

POV:

You survived. Barely. But at what cost?

Final Thoughts: So, Now What?

Stress is unavoidable, but how you handle it is what matters. Recognizing stress patterns helps you develop strategies to manage them effectively.

POV:

You now have scientific proof that being a college student is objectively stressful. But hey, at least you’re not alone in it.
